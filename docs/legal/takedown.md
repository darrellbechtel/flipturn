# Flip Turn — Data Takedown

Last updated: 2026-05-05
Contact: takedown@flipturn.app

If you are a swimmer, parent, or coach who wants athlete data removed from Flip Turn, this page is for you.

## How to request a takedown

Email **takedown@flipturn.app** with the following:

1. The **Swimming Canada (SNC) athlete ID(s)** you want removed. You can find this on results.swimming.ca on the athlete's profile page.
2. Your **relationship to the swimmer** (the swimmer themselves, parent or legal guardian, head coach of the swimmer's registered club, etc.).
3. A **brief reason** for the request — this is optional, but it helps us prioritize and improves the service. We do not require justification.

If you are emailing on behalf of a swimmer who is a minor, please confirm in your message that you are their parent or legal guardian.

## What happens next

- We will acknowledge your email within **2 business days**.
- We will remove the athlete data from Flip Turn within **7 days** of acknowledgement and reply to confirm the removal.
- Removal includes: the Athlete record, all scraped race results tied to that SNC ID, all `UserAthlete` links, and the raw scrape archive entries for that athlete.
- We will also block re-ingestion of the same SNC ID so the data does not come back the next time we refresh.

## What this does not do

The source data on **results.swimming.ca** is operated by Swimming Canada, not by Flip Turn. Removing data from our app does not remove anything from the public SNC archive, your provincial section's website, or any meet host's results page.

If you want your data removed from those sources, you need to contact Swimming Canada's privacy office directly. Their contact information is published on swimming.ca.

## Verification

For takedowns of minor athletes' data, we may ask for additional confirmation that you are the parent or guardian — for example, a club roster screenshot, a previous email between you and the swimmer's coach, or a similar low-burden check. We do **not** ask for government ID.

We process takedown requests in good faith and prioritize the swimmer's privacy. If there is any doubt about the legitimacy of a request, we will err on the side of removal.
